About me:

Greetings! I'm a technology consultant focusing on software architecture and scalable software engineering based out of Florida. Much of my work has been related, in some way, to natural language processing and large-scale data sets. I prefer working in functional programming languages, such as Clojure and Haskell; I also am proficient in Java.

Projects I've worked on generally have two main aspects:

First, I've designed and developed software to infer meaning from language (e.g., extraction of named entities or knowledge); this allows humans to more easily infer meaning from language data, especially with large data sets. For example, given a number of Tweets, how many mentions of CompanyX are there?

Second, I also support researchers and other software developers by developing data schemas for the representation of language. This facilitates collaboration and development of more advanced natural language processing toolkits. Many organizations struggle with disparate representations of data: having a unified schema allows progress on the organization's mission (improved advertisement penetration, for example) instead of engineering intra-company solutions to manage data.

I encourage you to view my work on Github or contact me with any questions you may have about my work or availability.
